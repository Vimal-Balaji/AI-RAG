The image describes a Halfwave Rectifier circuit converting AC signal to DC signal.

**Objects:** The diagram includes an AC Mains source, Step Down Transformer, Diode, and Load Resistor ($R_L$).

**Technical Content:** Rectification converts AC to DC. The process relies on diode biasing: during the positive half-cycle, the diode is forward biased, allowing output ($V_o$) to follow input. Conversely, during the negative half-cycle, the diode is reverse biased, blocking current flow.
**Axes Labels:** The graph uses $\omega t$ (time/angle) and Voltage (V).
